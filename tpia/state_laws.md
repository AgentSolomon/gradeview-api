# State Open Records Laws — GradeView Reference Guide

This file documents each state's open records law relevant to public university grade distribution requests.

**Request format to use:** "Grade distributions by course (course number and name), instructor name, semester/term, and year — in CSV or Excel format — for all undergraduate and graduate courses for the past 5 academic years. Please include instructor names and exclude section numbers. Do not aggregate or suppress individual sections unless required by FERPA."

---

## Alabama
- **Law:** Alabama Open Records Act (Ala. Code § 36-12-40)
- **Deadline:** No specific deadline; must be "within a reasonable time"
- **Notes:** No formal appeal process defined. Universities generally cooperative. No online submission portals common.

## Alaska
- **Law:** Alaska Public Records Act (AS 40.25.110)
- **Deadline:** 10 business days
- **Notes:** Must be Alaskan resident to request? No — federal FOIA applies regardless; Alaska law allows any person. Fees capped reasonably.

## Arizona
- **Law:** Arizona Public Records Law (A.R.S. § 39-121)
- **Deadline:** "Promptly" — typically 5–10 business days in practice
- **Notes:** ABOR (Arizona Board of Regents) institutions (ASU, UA, NAU) are subject. Many use NextRequest portal. No residency requirement.

## Arkansas
- **Law:** Arkansas Freedom of Information Act (Ark. Code Ann. § 25-19-101 et seq.)
- **Deadline:** 3 business days
- **Notes:** Strong law; must provide records within 3 business days (or acknowledge). No residency requirement.

## California
- **Law:** California Public Records Act (Cal. Gov. Code § 7920.000 et seq., formerly § 6250) — CPRA
- **Deadline:** 10 calendar days to respond; may extend 14 additional calendar days
- **Notes:** UC system (Berkeley, UCLA, Davis, San Diego, Santa Barbara, Irvine, Riverside, Santa Cruz, Merced) are subject. CSU system also subject. Many schools have online portals. Grade distributions are generally releasable with instructor names; FERPA does not protect aggregate data. Strong law.

## Colorado
- **Law:** Colorado Open Records Act (C.R.S. § 24-72-201 et seq.) — CORA
- **Deadline:** 3 business days (may extend to 7 for complex requests)
- **Notes:** CU system uses email/form per campus. Very accessible law.

## Connecticut
- **Law:** Connecticut Freedom of Information Act (Conn. Gen. Stat. § 1-200 et seq.) — FOIA
- **Deadline:** 4 business days
- **Notes:** Very strong law. FOI Commission enforces. UConn system subject.

## Delaware
- **Law:** Delaware Freedom of Information Act (29 Del. C. § 10001 et seq.) — FOIA
- **Deadline:** 15 business days
- **Notes:** University of Delaware is public and subject.

## Florida
- **Law:** Florida Public Records Law (Chapter 119, Florida Statutes) — "Sunshine Law"
- **Deadline:** No specific deadline — "reasonable time" required; Florida courts have interpreted this strictly
- **Notes:** One of the strongest open records laws in the nation. No residency requirement. Cannot deny based on purpose. Universities must provide records promptly. Many Florida universities use NextRequest, JustFOIA, or GovQA portals. Grade distributions with instructor names are releasable as they are not student-identifying.

## Georgia
- **Law:** Georgia Open Records Act (O.C.G.A. § 50-18-70 et seq.) — ORA
- **Deadline:** 3 business days to acknowledge; records within a "reasonable time" (typically 3–10 business days)
- **Notes:** Strong law. USG (University System of Georgia) institutions all subject. No residency requirement.

## Hawaii
- **Law:** Hawaii Uniform Information Practices Act (HRS § 92F) — UIPA
- **Deadline:** 10 business days
- **Notes:** University of Hawaii system subject. Fee waivers possible for public interest.

## Idaho
- **Law:** Idaho Public Records Act (Idaho Code § 74-101 et seq.)
- **Deadline:** 3 business days (may extend to 10)
- **Notes:** Boise State, Idaho State, University of Idaho all subject.

## Illinois
- **Law:** Illinois Freedom of Information Act (5 ILCS 140) — FOIA
- **Deadline:** 5 business days (may extend 5 more for "unduly burdensome" requests)
- **Notes:** University of Illinois System handles all UIUC, UIC, and UIS requests centrally via foia@uillinois.edu. Other state universities handle independently.

## Indiana
- **Law:** Indiana Access to Public Records Act (Ind. Code § 5-14-3) — APRA
- **Deadline:** 24 hours for certain records; generally within 7 business days
- **Notes:** One of the faster laws. IU, Purdue, IUPUI, Ball State, Indiana State all subject. No residency requirement.

## Iowa
- **Law:** Iowa Open Records Law (Iowa Code Chapter 22)
- **Deadline:** "Reasonable time" — no fixed deadline, but must be "prompt"
- **Notes:** State Board of Regents institutions (UI, Iowa State, UNI) subject. University of Iowa charges $75/hr database extraction fee — ask for cost estimate first.

## Kansas
- **Law:** Kansas Open Records Act (K.S.A. § 45-215 et seq.) — KORA
- **Deadline:** 3 business days
- **Notes:** KU, Kansas State, Wichita State subject. Strong law.

## Kentucky
- **Law:** Kentucky Open Records Act (KRS 61.870–61.991)
- **Deadline:** 3 business days
- **Notes:** UK, U of Louisville, WKU, Murray State, Eastern KY, Morehead State, NKU all subject. Good law.

## Louisiana
- **Law:** Louisiana Public Records Law (La. R.S. § 44:1 et seq.)
- **Deadline:** 3 business days
- **Notes:** LSU system, Tulane (private — not subject), Southern University, UNO all apply. Strong law.

## Maine
- **Law:** Maine Freedom of Access Act (1 M.R.S. § 401 et seq.) — FOAA
- **Deadline:** 5 business days
- **Notes:** University of Maine System subject.

## Maryland
- **Law:** Maryland Public Information Act (Md. Code, Gen. Prov. § 4-101 et seq.) — PIA
- **Deadline:** 10 business days (may extend 30 more with notice)
- **Notes:** University of Maryland system institutions subject. Strong law with fee waiver provisions.

## Massachusetts
- **Law:** Massachusetts Public Records Law (M.G.L. c. 66, § 10)
- **Deadline:** 10 business days
- **Notes:** UMass system subject. Recent reforms (2016) strengthened the law. Note: Private universities like Harvard, MIT, Tufts are NOT subject.

## Michigan
- **Law:** Michigan Freedom of Information Act (MCL 15.231 et seq.) — FOIA
- **Deadline:** 5 business days (may extend 10 more)
- **Notes:** U of Michigan, MSU, Wayne State, Western Michigan, Central Michigan, Eastern Michigan, Ferris State, Grand Valley, Northern Michigan, Lake Superior State, Michigan Tech, Oakland, Saginaw Valley all subject.

## Minnesota
- **Law:** Minnesota Government Data Practices Act (Minn. Stat. Chapter 13) — MGDPA
- **Deadline:** "Reasonable time" — no fixed deadline; in practice 5–10 business days
- **Notes:** University of Minnesota system subject. MnSCU schools (Minnesota State University system) subject. Aggregate grade data generally public.

## Mississippi
- **Law:** Mississippi Public Records Act (Miss. Code Ann. § 25-61-1 et seq.)
- **Deadline:** 7 business days
- **Notes:** Ole Miss, Mississippi State, USM, Jackson State all subject.

## Missouri
- **Law:** Missouri Sunshine Law (RSMo Chapter 610)
- **Deadline:** 3 business days to acknowledge; records within 10 business days (may extend)
- **Notes:** UM System handles all requests via system office. Portal available at umsystem.edu.

## Montana
- **Law:** Montana Constitution Art. II § 9; Montana Code Ann. § 2-6-1003
- **Deadline:** "Reasonable time" — typically 5–10 business days
- **Notes:** University of Montana, Montana State subject.

## Nebraska
- **Law:** Nebraska Public Records Statutes (Neb. Rev. Stat. § 84-712 et seq.)
- **Deadline:** 4 business days (may extend 16 more)
- **Notes:** University of Nebraska system subject. UNL, UNO, UNMC, UNK.

## Nevada
- **Law:** Nevada Public Records Act (NRS 239.001 et seq.)
- **Deadline:** 5 business days (may extend 15 more)
- **Notes:** NSHE (Nevada System of Higher Education) institutions subject: UNLV, UNR, etc.

## New Hampshire
- **Law:** New Hampshire Right-to-Know Law (RSA 91-A)
- **Deadline:** 5 business days
- **Notes:** UNH system subject.

## New Jersey
- **Law:** New Jersey Open Public Records Act (N.J.S.A. 47:1A-1 et seq.) — OPRA
- **Deadline:** 7 business days
- **Notes:** Rutgers is the primary public research university. NJIT, Montclair State, Kean, Rowan, TCNJ also subject.

## New Mexico

- **Deadline:** 15 calendar days
- **Notes:** UNM, NM State, NM Tech, Eastern NM, Western NM all subject.

## New York

- **Deadline:** 5 business days to acknowledge; must decide within 20 business days
- **Notes:** SUNY system (64 campuses) all subject. Each campus has its own Records Access Officer. CUNY system also subject. No residency requirement.

## North Carolina

- **Deadline:** No fixed deadline — "as promptly as possible"
- **Notes:** UNC System schools all subject. Many use NextRequest portal. Aggregate grade distribution data with instructor names is releasable.

## North Dakota
- **Law:** North Dakota Open Records Law (N.D.C.C. § 44-04-18 et seq.)
- **Deadline:** "Reasonable time"
- **Notes:** UND, NDSU subject.

## Ohio
- **Law:** Ohio Public Records Act (ORC Chapter 149)
- **Deadline:** "Promptly" — Ohio courts interpret this strictly; typically within 5 business days
- **Notes:** Extremely strong law. No residency requirement. Ohio State, Ohio University, Kent State, U of Cincinnati, Miami, Bowling Green, Toledo, Akron, Wright State, Cleveland State, Shawnee, Youngstown State all subject.

## Oklahoma
- **Law:** Oklahoma Open Records Act (51 O.S. § 24A.1 et seq.)

- **Notes:** OU, OSU, OU Health Sciences, UCO, East Central, Northeastern, Northwestern, Southeastern, Southwestern, Cameron, Langston, Rogers State, Connors State all subject.

## Oregon

- **Deadline:** 5 business days (may extend 25 more for complex requests)
- **Notes:** U of Oregon, Oregon State, Portland State, Western Oregon, Eastern Oregon, Southern Oregon, OIT all subject.

## Pennsylvania

- **Deadline:** 5 business days (may extend 30 more)
- **Notes:** **IMPORTANT: Penn State, Pitt, Lincoln, and Temple are "state-related" institutions — they are NOT subject to the RTKL.** They receive state appropriations but are technically private corporations. Pennsylvania's purely state-owned schools (like Bloomsburg, Millersville, East Stroudsburg, etc. — the Pennsylvania State System of Higher Education / PASSHE schools) ARE subject.

## Key Notes for Grade Distribution Requests

1. **FERPA vs. Open Records:** FERPA protects *individual student* education records, not aggregate data. Grade distributions by course/instructor do not identify individual students and are generally NOT protected by FERPA. Universities often try to cite FERPA incorrectly — push back.

2. **Format:** Always request CSV or Excel explicitly. Universities are not required to create new records, but if they maintain electronic grade databases (they all do), producing a report in CSV format is not "creating" a new record — it's extracting existing data.

3. **Instructor Names:** Instructor names are employee information, not student information, and are generally public. Some states (notably California) protect certain employee privacy, but job-related performance data like course assignments are public.

4. **Cost:** Some universities charge database extraction fees. University of Iowa charges $75/hr. Request a cost estimate before proceeding. You can argue that a simple SQL query should take minimal time.

5. **Pennsylvania Exception:** Penn State, Pitt, Temple, and Lincoln University are NOT subject to Pennsylvania RTKL. They are "state-related" institutions with limited disclosure requirements.

6. **Private Universities:** Not subject to any state FOIA. Harvard, MIT, Stanford, Yale, Princeton, Columbia, USC, NYU, Duke, Vanderbilt, Emory, Georgetown, etc. — skip entirely.
