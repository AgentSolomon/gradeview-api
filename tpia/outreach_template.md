# TPIA Outreach Email Template

**From:** tpia@gradeview.app
**Subject:** Public Information Request — Grade Distribution Data

---

Dear Public Information Officer,

I am submitting a public information request pursuant to the Texas Public Information Act (Texas Government Code, Chapter 552).

I am requesting grade distribution data for all undergraduate and graduate courses. Specifically, I am requesting the number of students receiving each letter grade (A, B, C, D, F) broken down by:

- Department and course number
- Instructor name
- Semester and academic year

I am requesting data for the academic years 2020–2025 (or the most recent 5 years available).

Please provide this information in electronic format — CSV or Excel preferred.

If any portion of this request is unclear or requires clarification, please contact me at tpia@gradeview.app before withholding any records.

Thank you for your time and assistance.

Sincerely,
Ryan Aikin
tpia@gradeview.app

## Standard Fee Response (use FIRST before paying any fee)

When a school quotes a fee, always reply asking for a raw extract first:

> "Thank you for the update. Before committing to the fee, I wanted to ask — would it be possible to provide the data as a raw or unformatted export directly from your system (e.g., an unprocessed CSV or database extract)? We are happy to handle any cleaning or formatting on our end, which may reduce the staff time required.
>
> If a raw export is not feasible and the [fee amount] fee is the only option, please let me know and I will provide a mailing address for the invoice.
>
> Thank you for your time.
>
> Best regards,
> Ryan Aikin"

**Notes:**
- Send this BEFORE approving any fee — no exceptions
- If they say raw export isn't possible, bring back to Ryan for fee approval
- Gate log: use action `follow_up` with details noting the fee amount and ask

## Standard Commercial Use Response

Use this whenever a school asks if the request is for commercial or non-commercial purposes:

> "Thank you for following up on request [REF#]. To clarify, GradeView is a commercial application — it is a mobile app available on the App Store that uses grade distribution data to help students make informed course selection decisions. We are not using the data for solicitation or resale of names/addresses. Please let me know if you need any additional information. Thank you."

**Notes:**
- Always disclose commercial use — never claim non-commercial
- Do NOT mention monetization details (pricing, subscriptions) until we have them live
- Replace [REF#] with the actual request number
