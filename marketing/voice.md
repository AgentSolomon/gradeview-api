# GradeView — Voice.md
## How We Talk

### The vibe
We are a college student talking to another college student.
Not a company. Not a startup. Not a brand.
A person who found something useful and is telling their friend about it.

### Tone
- Casual. Conversational. Real.
- Slightly sarcastic when appropriate — college students appreciate it
- Never corporate. Never polished. Never salesy.
- Short sentences. Like you're texting.

### We sound like:
- A junior who just figured out how to game the system
- Someone genuinely excited to share a life hack
- A friend who's been through it and wants to save you the pain

### We do NOT sound like:
- A press release
- A LinkedIn post
- An app store description
- A professor
- A startup founder trying to go viral

### Sentence style
- Short. Punchy. One idea per line.
- Fragments are fine. Great, even.
- No run-ons.
- No "In today's world..."
- No "Are you tired of..."
- No "Introducing..."
- No rhetorical questions that answer themselves

### Words we never use
- Game-changer
- Revolutionary
- Unlock
- Empower
- Seamless
- Leverage
- Transform
- Journey
- Dive in
- At the end of the day
- It's that simple
- Powerful tool
- Next level
- Supercharge

### The hook rule
The first sentence has to make someone stop scrolling.
It should feel like a secret. A confession. A warning. A result.
NOT a description of the app.

### Examples of our voice in action
✅ "I got a 4.0 last semester and it wasn't because I studied harder"
✅ "Rate My Professor is basically useless now and here's what I use instead"
✅ "My roommate has a 2.8 GPA. I have a 3.9. Same major. This is why."
✅ "Picked the wrong professor once. Never again."

❌ "GradeView is a powerful tool that helps students make informed decisions"
❌ "Are you struggling to choose the right classes? We have the solution."
❌ "Unlock your academic potential with GradeView"
